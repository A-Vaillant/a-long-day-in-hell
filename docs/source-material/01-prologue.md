# Prologue

## Summary

Soren narrates from unfathomable eons in the future. He's alone, has been alone for ages. He has climbed "innumerable light-years" from bottom to current level. His memory is perfect and cannot forget any detail. He has found treasures — near-matches to real books, coherent fragments, a Mickey Spillane approximation he carried for 700 billion years. One book speculated that God created the universe as a sorting algorithm for finding meaningful books in the library (evolution as search function).

He introduces his framing: he found *this* book — close enough to his story to submit, though with "slight variations." He sets up telling the story from the beginning.

## Key Prose & Imagery

> "there has been only one genuine love in my near-eternally stretched life – Rachel who fell to the bottom of the library without me"

> "I still listen for the sound of another's voice, the ring of footsteps on the stairs, or a figure moving silhouetted in the distance. Once I spent a year just listening."

> "I tried to build a telescope made from clarified sheep intestines from the kiosk"

> "Sometimes I fantasize I will discover the book that describes the location of the volume I have been searching for. But alas, how would I know it was the right one?"

> "a brief love can structure and define the very topology of our consciousness ever after"

## Implementable Details

- **Perfect memory**: Hell gives perfect recall of every moment of earthly life. "This was to be the greatest curse of Hell." We already have this — player remembers life story.
- **Listening for others**: "the ring of footsteps on the stairs, or a figure moving silhouetted in the distance" — direct source material for ambient NPC events.
- **Near-miss books**: books that are *close* to meaningful. A biography of his digestive history. Stories that almost work. We have proximity/fragment system already.
- **Telescope from kiosk materials**: kiosk gives any food, which means raw materials (intestines, bones). People get creative.
- **Loneliness as primary condition**: after long enough, isolation is total.
- **Coherent text as treasure**: finding real words is a major event even after billions of years.
