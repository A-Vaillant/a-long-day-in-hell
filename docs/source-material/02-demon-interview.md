# The Demon Interview

## Summary

Five people in white robes on metal folding chairs before the demon Xandern (a Yazata, minor functionary). Elaborate office with faux lake-of-fire window (actors, they get off in half an hour). The demon assigns each person to a personalized Hell by tapping on a red rectangle (tablet). Zoroastrianism is the one true religion; everyone else goes to Hell, but it's finite — you eventually get out.

Lester the arrogant Christian gets sent somewhere ironic. Julia the atheist biologist gets sent based on her bumblebee expertise. Others dispatched quickly. Soren — Mormon, geologist, father of four, died of brain cancer at 45, "liked to read" — gets the Library.

## Key Prose & Imagery

> "Not Zoroastrian either then?" — recurring greeting between strangers

> "The monster's yellow gaze was directed thoughtfully out of the large framed window ... a large cavern lit with a dancing red glow"

> The demon's critique of eternal Hell: "Most of you wouldn't do that to a neighbor's dog, even if it barked incessantly at two a.m. every morning."

> "'Injustice?' queried the demon sarcastically. 'You were never concerned with justice a day in your life except when it was in your favor.'"

> "Suddenly he gave a chuckle and sighed. 'Oh, why not? The Great God created irony too.'"

> On Soren: "you liked to read ... in fact it seems you loved books. Interesting."

## Implementable Details

- **The demon is personable, darkly funny**, not menacing. Our game's tone should match: dry, ironic, slightly bureaucratic horror.
- **Personalized Hells**: the library is specifically for a reader. The cruelty is tailored.
- **The lava scene is fake**: "actors." Everything in Hell might be constructed/managed. Raises questions about what's real.
- **"Not Zoroastrian either then?"**: could be NPC greeting dialogue.
- **Red rectangle (tablet)**: the demon uses technology. Hell has an IT department.
- **Marriage voided**: rule #6. Soren's wife is gone. This is a core wound.
