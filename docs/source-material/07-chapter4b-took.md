# Chapter 4 (cont): Mr. Took's Calculation

## Summary

Soren climbs up for weeks after his fall. Finds people in deep mourning. Mr. Took — a mathematician — has calculated the number of books: 95^1,312,000. The dimensions of the library: 10^1,297,377 light-years to the bottom from the middle. More orders of magnitude in light-years than there were electrons in the old universe.

"Rachel!" Soren cries. "I'll never get to the bottom." The mathematician laughs bitterly: "Oh. You'll reach bottom. Just not for a very, very long time."

The mourning is collective — people are catatonic, weeping, hollowed out. The scale of the task has been made real.

> "'Ninety-five raised to the one million three hundred twelve thousandth power.' 'That's a lot. Right?'"

> "'In our old universe there were only ten raised to the seventy-eighth electrons.'"

> "His cold laugh."

> People's reaction: "Some seemed almost catatonic, staring into nothing, their faces a frozen rictus empty of expression."

> "'You mean there are more books in this library than there were electrons in our whole previous universe?' 'Way more.'"

## Implementable Details

- **The calculation as despair trigger**: learning the true scale destroys hope. This could be an event or a Zoroastrian text variant — the posted text at each floor could include the math.
- **Catatonic NPCs**: "staring into nothing, their faces a frozen rictus." Our NPC disposition system has "catatonic" as a state. This is what it looks like.
- **Collective mourning**: one person's discovery affects everyone. Information spreads. NPC dialogue could reference "the calculation" or "Took's numbers."
- **The bitter laugh**: dark humor as coping mechanism. NPC mad/anxious dialogue should include this — gallows humor.
