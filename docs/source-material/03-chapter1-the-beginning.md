# Chapter 1: The Beginning

## Summary

Soren arrives disoriented but physically perfect — age 25, teeth fixed, cancer gone, wedding ring gone. Drops to his knees to pray but is overwhelmed with doubt (praying to whom?). Lights go out his first night; he weeps in the dark.

Morning: he surveys the library for the first time. The chasm, the railing, floors extending infinitely up and down, people scattered everywhere — "some standing, staring blankly, others walking in a daze, some weeping uncontrollably, some kneeling in desperate prayer." Finds rest areas with clocks, kiosks, bedrooms (7 beds), bathrooms. The clock reads "Year 0000000, Day 2."

The sign with the rules. The kiosk gives any food ("a vanilla shake, like the kind at the Purple Aardvark on Second South"). Meets Elliott (agnostic, died at 93), Larisa (died of breast cancer at 54). Group hug, then laughter, then sobs. "We were all strangers, all lost and alone, and the absurdity of our situation struck us."

First night: absolute silence. "Not even the whisper of any air movement. The stillness of the grave." Kiosk doesn't work when lights are off.

> "Looking across the railing to the other side of the chasm, which seemed as bottomless as the corridor seemed long"

> On the food: "'Could I get a diet Coke, too?' It appeared likewise."

## The Rules (verbatim from the sign)

1. Please be kind. Failure brings unhappiness.
2. Do not get discouraged. Nothing lasts forever.
3. Towels on floor = cleaned. Hung up = reuse.
4. Books not in your possession return to shelves every night. Possession = touching it.
5. If killed, restored to life the following day. Try to avoid death.
6. All prior contracts/bonds/commitments null and void (including marriage).
7. You are never really alone (though it may feel like it).
8. Don't write on/mark/mar library materials. Repairs made nightly.
9. You are here to learn something. Don't try to figure out what it is.
