4. [Chapter 2: First Week](04-chapter2-first-week.md) — Throwing books. Biscuit explains the Library of Babel. The run (1400 mi, no end). Alcohol. Betty. Moral collapse.

## Recurring Themes for Implementation

### The Soundscape of Hell
- Footsteps on stairs (prologue)
- Absolute silence at night (ch1)
- "The clomping of my feet" as poverty of sensation (ch4)
- "The ring of footsteps ... a figure moving silhouetted in the distance" (prologue)
- Improvised music: bone flutes, gut harps (ch3)

### Coherent Text as Treasure
- "sack it" — two words, a man weeps (ch2)
- "Can dye dogs riverward" — MST runner-up (ch3)
- "The bat housed again four leaves of it" — annual ceremony (ch3)
- "Breath, comes to me in bursts of joy..." — two sentences, tears (ch4)
- "catch trees as windy dots" — found after 144 years alone (ch5)
- "lightbulb ocean left" — MST of year 25 (ch3)

### The Chasm
- Bottomless from above (ch1)
- V8 can dropped, no sound (ch1)
- Rachel's jump (ch4)
- Freefall physics, skydiving, smock-as-wing (ch4)
- Thirst/starvation during fall (ch4)
- Crashing back in — brutal, multi-day, limb-losing (ch4, ch5)
- Spinning technique with Wand (ch5)

### NPC Disposition Arc (matches our calm→anxious→mad→catatonic→dead)
- Arrival: confused, prayerful, weeping (ch1)
- Early days: bonding, humor, drinking, exploration (ch2)
- Year 100: exhaustion, "I'm sick of it," breakdowns (ch3)
- Year 1000+: relationships dissolve, silence (ch3)
- Direites: madness, religious violence (ch4)
- Catatonic: "staring into nothing, faces a frozen rictus" (ch4b)
- Deep time: "I ceased to think, to perceive" (ch5)

### Things Hell Doesn't Have
- Nature (no animals, plants, weather, wind)
- Diversity (all white American English speakers)
- Night sounds (total silence)
- Meaning ("all meaning has been subtracted")
- Dreams / things to look forward to
- Context for moral decisions

### The Kiosk
- Any food, any specificity, any cuisine
- Raw materials (bones, intestines, coconut fiber)
- Alcohol works, addiction doesn't
- No cigarettes
- Off when lights are out
